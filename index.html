Dr. Thomas Sowell

The professor of economics who started the black exit of the Democratic party

Here's a time line of Dr. Sowell's life:

  • 1930 - Born in Gastonia, North Carolina
  • 1939 - Moves to Harlem with his great Aunt Molly, after the death of his mother and father.
  • 1946 - Though improvished, his middle school performance and IQ test permitted him to the prestigius Stuyvesant High School. By the age of sixteen, he dropped out of high school, moved out of his aunt's home, and took various low-income jobs to help the family.
  • Late 1940s - With the little money from his Western Union job delivering telegrams, he'd ride the fare from Fifth Avenue and tour the most affluent areas of Manhatten.
    • “Marx explained the world around me,” Sowell explained.
  • 1950 - Young Sowell became attracted to the philosophy of the socialist Karl Marx.

  • 1951 - Sowell is drafted into the Marine Corps during the Korean War. He faced many challenges like racial discrimination, but found fulfillment and his passion in photography.
  • 1958 - After his military discharging, he transfered from Howard University to Harvard university for a challenge. Later on, he graduated magna cum laude in economics.
    • - Sowell completes his doctoral fellowship under free market economist, Milton Friedman. It was in Professior Friendman's price theory class in which he read Friedrich von Hayek’s essay “The Use of Knowledge in Society.” This left an impact on how fragmented data and knowledge is decentralized amongst people and how this shaped decision making for the free market system.
  • 1960 - It was during his internship at the US Department of Labor, Sowell's faith in the role of government and government programs changed. This was also the time his views on Marxism fiercely changed. He was assigned to study the impacts of the historical US minimum wage laws in the sugar cane industry of Puerto Rico. At the time there was a rise in the unemployment rates and declining sugar cane production. Leading experts speculated this was due to hurricanes.
    • “I came into the office one day, and I announced to the whole group: ‘What we need are statistics on the amount of sugarcane standing in the fields before the hurricane came through,’” Sowell recalled. “I am waiting for the congratulations and I could see looks of shock in the room: ‘This idiot has stumbled on something that could ruin us all.’ I was concerned whether this law was beneficial or not beneficial to low-income people. They were concerned because this law was providing one-third of the income of the US Department of Labor.”
  • 1968 - Before graduating with his PhD at Columbia, Sowell wrote his thesis on the historical analysis on Say's law
  • 1972-74 - Sowell is the project director at the Urban Institue
  • 1975 - Adjunct scholar of the American Enterprise Institue
  • 76-77 - Sowell becomes a fellow at the Center for Advanced Study in the Behavioral Sciences at Stanford University
  • 1980 - Sowell became a senior fellow of the Hoover Instition, a Stanford University public policy think tank that focueses on individual, economic, and political freedom. He has his first book published, Knowledge and Decisions , which expands on Hayek’s article "The Use of Knowledge in Society"
  • 1987 - Sowell publishes A Conflict of Visions which outlines the patterns in the source of generational and centuries long-political controversies. It discusses about the utopian versus the tragic view of man-kind.
  • 1999 - The Quest for Cosmic Justice is published
  • 2002 - Sowell is awared with the National Humanities Medel
  • 2008 - Sowell publishes Economic Facts and Fallacies, which 'exposes some of the most popular fallacies about economic issues.'
  • 2009 - Professor Sowell publishes an economic books including Housing Boom and Bust and Intellectuals and Society
  • 2018 - His latest publication Discrimination and Disparities gathers the facts to challenge the idea that economic outcomes are a result from racial discrimination and exploitation.
